The Intent

Day 01  ·  April 18, 2026

Arrival & Zen

📍 Bisma Street at Sunset

First Light in a Foreign Stillness

The flight from Changi was barely two and a half hours, but landing in Bali always feels like crossing a threshold — from the hum of productivity into something older and quieter. The guesthouse on Bisma Street revealed itself at golden hour, tucked behind a low stone wall and a curtain of bougainvillea.

The town was quieter than expected for April. Somewhere in the middle distance, a gamelan. No WiFi for the first hour, which turned out to be the best possible beginning.

Note to self: the vegetarian nasi goreng at the warung across from the rice field terrace is worth every rupiah. Order a second portion.

Day 03  ·  April 20, 2026

Nature Flow

📍 Campuhan Ridge Walk, 6 AM

The Ridge Before the World Woke Up

Alarm at 5:15. The air at that hour is something else entirely — cool, dense with the smell of wet earth and frangipani, and utterly without other people. The Campuhan Ridge trail is narrow, flanked by alang-alang grass that brushes your arms as you walk toward the horizon.

Three pages of longhand notes completed before breakfast. Something about moving through physical space unlocks whatever stays blocked at a desk. The body thinks differently when it moves.

Photography lesson of the day: the best light lasted exactly nineteen minutes. Next time, arrive twenty minutes earlier and stay nineteen minutes later.

Day 06  ·  April 23, 2026

Artistic Drift

📍 Tegalalang Rice Terraces

The Architecture of Patience

Tegalalang is a reminder that the most beautiful things take generations to build. The paddies weren't designed — they accumulated, layer by layer, each carved by hand to catch water and light just so. Looking at them long enough, you start to see it as a living system, not a landscape.

Spent the morning doing compositional sketches. Perspective keeps shifting the further you descend. By the time I'd walked to the valley floor, the light overhead had turned fully golden.

Bought a small watercolor of the terraces from a local artist at the base. Couldn't not. It cost less than a coffee in Singapore and will outlast most things I own.

Day 08  ·  April 25, 2026

Reflection

📍 Saraswati Water Palace

Still Water, Still Mind

The lotus flowers at Saraswati open slowly — not dramatically, just steadily. I sat at the edge of the reflecting pool for almost an hour. Nobody asked me to leave. Nobody asked me anything at all. This is, I think, the exact thing I came here for.

Tomorrow is departure day. The notebook is almost full. I don't think that's a coincidence — the place and the time conspired to make me write more honestly than I normally allow myself.

"To travel is to notice. To write is to notice that you noticed." — Something I scribbled between the second and third coffee, which may or may not be original.
